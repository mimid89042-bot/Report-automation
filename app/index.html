Case 1 loading

These loading conditions may apply when the rig or crane operator is unlikely to be able to aid recovery from an imminent platform failure. Operations in which this type of loading condition applies could include:

• Standing

• Travelling

• Handling (in crane mode, e.g. lifting a precast concrete pile into the leader, handling casings and reinforcement cages)

Case 2 loading

These loading conditions may apply when the rig or crane operator can control the load safely, for example by releasing the line load, or by reducing power, to aid recovery from an imminent platform failure. Case 2 loading is generally given by the worst combination of loas and orientation of the rig. Operations in which this type of loading condition applies to a piling rig could include:

• Installing casing

• Drilling

• Extracting an auger

• Extracting casing

• Rig travelling or slewing with a fixed mast which has a foot or fixed load (e.g. pile held in the leader) close to the platform surface
